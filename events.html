ENERGY GENNEXT

ENERGY GENNEXT

"The environmental problem is a global one, & only global action will resolve it”, these are the words from Biologist, Barry Commoner. This workshop revolved around the concept of renewable energy and was held by by GreenAdd in collaboration with the 2°C Challenge Communiqué, University of Cambridge. It was held over two days and included four modules- Solar Tracking (Using Solar Cells), Hybrid Electric Vehicle, two models of Wind turbines & Hydrogen Fuel Cell technical.

On day one the students were given practical model of energy generating devices and were taught to assemble it. Solar wind hybrid mechanics with Balance of systems was explained, so that positioning of the turbines could be done correctly and more energy could be produced. Participants made the Chinese wind turbine, a horizontal axis wind turbine. These were tested by installing pedestal fans to provide the necessary wind for demonstration. Assembling the turbines was the most exciting part for the participants. Later on they were taught about the different types of solar cells, and the arrangement for tracking sunlight, so that the sun’s energy could be trapped and used for various purposes in our daily life.

On the second day we aimed on tracking vehicles, which can potentially be replaced by its "smarter & e-friendly" version called Hybrid Electric Vehicle. The name Hybrid comes from the fact that it can work on conventional fuels & renewable energy sources too. The students got a practical experience of making one. Hybrid Electric Vehicle is a vehicle that uses two (or more) distinct power sources to move the vehicle. It consists of a combined internal combustion engine & 1/more electric motors. The power sources include:-

-On board/ out board Rechargeable Energy Storage System (RESS)

-Solar, Wind -Coal, Wood, Petrol, Diesel

The students at the end of the two day workshop took an oath to help conserve energy and reuse energy how much is possible. With 100% hands on practical experience, the students got an insight into renewable resources and ways to generate it.

WIRED ROBOTICS

WIRED ROBOTICS

In Robotics Level 1 Wired Bot workshop students were taught how to build a wired robotic car and also the working and function of each component used. It was an entertaining event. It required no prior technical knowledge except basic tool handling skills. All the technical knowhow required for the event was given by the event heads and the volunteers.

They were a total of 32 teams, each team consisting of not more than 4 participants. The event panned for two days. The students were taught to assemble the chassis of the car and wiring of the motors on the first day. It included attaching the tires, attaching the motors and connecting wires through connectors. On the second day, participants were taught the console connections for controlling the motors. It was a tricky part as it required a lot of soldering of wires in which the volunteers were very helpful.

In the end all the bots were functioning. The participants were very happy with the efforts and the response as well as feedback was very positive with a demand for Level 2 Wireless Robotics Workshop.

CONQUEST

CONQUEST

 

“CONQUEST” was organised as an opportunity for the First Year Engineering students to interact with the fellow mates in their new college. This required them to go beyond their comfort zone and   mingle with their batch mates on a ‘real and not virtual networking platform’. A total of 140 First Year students gathered with their teeming enthusiasm to be greeted by their seniors from all branches and years.

The basic idea of the event was to get the FEs out of their shell by enabling them to know people around them be it seniors or batch mates and likewise for their seniors. This was accomplished by   making a group of four FEs and assigning them with a group volunteer from the succeeding tasks. This facilitated interaction between the new students and their seniors. Every group was to compete against other groups over a set of definite designed events. This was to inculcate competition spirit in them and most importantly team spirit and bonding.

Being an engineering college, the events designed were ‘techno events’ or technical events. From ‘Buzz Wire’ which set the task of passing a ring through a closed copper wire circuit without touching it , i.e. without a ‘buzz sound’ to ‘Motherboard Component Naming’ which tested them on basic component knowledge. From solving time-constrained mathematical problems of ‘GOFigure’ to ‘4hints one word’ which was a technical riddle,  the students used utmost of their team’s intellect ,be it ‘ Brainvita’ or ‘Robo car Racing’ or situational ‘story making’ which involved the use of presence of mind of team mates with their thinking in sync to complete a random story. To avoid the monotony, some fun events were also added like ‘Tic Tac Toe with a crazy ball’ and tricky ‘Color Naming Games’ where the participants were supposed to read out the color of words which also happened to be the names of different colors.

The event ended up with the students having a whale of time. The creative ones got a chance to display their skills while designing an “IEEE logo and tagline” offering their novel perspective with respect to IEEE, and thereby leaving their seniors absolutely impressed. With all this in action we saw how our motive of getting FEs develop their own ground in their college came to fruition when the  students left with their new found friends, sportiness and a smile to last for us and them, alike.

PHOTOSHOP

PHOTOSHOP

This was an event organized by IEEE RGIT in collaboration with ABIT. It was a highly informative and interactive workshop which saw a turnout of over 120 students from all the streams with the lab being completely occupied.

FL STUDIO

FL STUDIO (coming soon)

FL Studio (formerly known as Fruity Loops) is a digital audio workstation developed by the Belgian company Image-Line. FL Studio features a graphical user interface based on a pattern-based music sequencer.

The program is available in four different editions for Microsoft Windows, including FL Studio Express, Fruity Edition, Producer Edition, and the Signature Bundle. Image-Line offers lifetime free updates to the program, meaning customers receive all future updates of the software for free. Image-Line also develops FL Studio Mobile for iPod Touch, iPhone, iPad and Android devices.

It is a complete software music production environment or DAW( Digital Audio Workshop). Everything you need is in one package to compose, arrange,record, edit, mix and master professional quality music. It is the fastest way from your brain to your speakers.

FL Studio can also be used as a VST instrument in other audio workstation programs and also functions as a ReWire client. Image-Line also offers other VST instruments and audio applications. FL Studio is used by electronic musicians and DJs such as Afrojack, Avicii, Martin Garrix and 9th Wonder.

We are soon planning to organise an event to teach F L Studio to the students of our college to enhance their knowledge about composing music. Details will be disclosed soon.

GAMING

GAMING (coming soon)

A platform game (or platformer) is a video game which involves guiding an avatar to jump between suspended platforms, over obstacles, or both to advance the game. These challenges are known as jumping puzzles or freerunning. The player controls the jumps to avoid letting the avatar fall from platforms or miss necessary jumps. The most common unifying element of games of this genre is the jump button. Jumping, in this genre, may include swinging from extendable arm or bouncing from springboards or trampolines. These mechanics, even in the context of other genres, are commonly called platforming, a verbification of platform. Games where jumping is automated completely, fall outside of the genre.

While commonly associated with console gaming, there have been many important platform games released to video arcades, as well as for handheld game consoles and home computers. North America, Europe and Japan have played major parts in the genre's evolution. Platform themes range from cartoon-like games to science fiction and fantasy epics

UPSANA MAKATI

UPSANA MAKATI

Entrepreneurship is not only a means of making money,  true entrepreneurs always have humanity in the core of his/her idea, they mix profit with social revolution for the benefit of mankind and for the advantage of less-privileged. Ms.‘Upasana Makati’ is one amongst them.

The event commenced with a brief introduction and felicitation of the chief guest Ms.Upasana Makati. She introduced herself as a 24 year old entrepreneur and spoke about her college days in Jai Hind, where she studied media, management etc. She shared her experiences and thoughts with one and all saying that even though she enjoyed her job at the work place, she felt something amiss as she wasn’t actively contributing to the society. She came up with India’s first English lifestyle magazine in Braille. In a bid to reach out to the visually impaired population of India, Upasana Makati decided to launch White Print, an English lifestyle magazine in Braille. She never thought of doing a ‘first’ in India, but her braille magazine is one of a kind. She refers to them as ‘visually impaired’ rather than ‘blind’. They had nothing enjoyable as a magazine or newspaper. Driven by noble thoughts, she strived to change this state. White Print is an initiative born to cater to the needs of the visually impaired community. Ms. Makati explained how people voluntarily wrote articles for the magazine, Barkha Dutt being one of the prominent names. The magazine consists of about 30 pages and now has over 300 subscribers. It is a monthly magazine covering topics such as food, politics, music and gadget reviews. She has plans to publish it in regional languages as time progresses.
